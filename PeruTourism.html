Machu Picchu

A UNESCO World Heritage site, Machu Picchu is an ancient Inca citadel set high in the Andes Mountains.

Cusco

The historic city of Cusco was the capital of the Inca Empire and is now a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Its charming streets are lined with colonial architecture and archaeological wonders.

Lake Titicaca

Situated on the border between Peru and Bolivia, Lake Titicaca is one of the highest navigable lakes in the world. The floating Uros Islands and the tranquil Amantani Island are popular destinations on the lake.

Arequipa

Known as the "White City" for its stunning white volcanic stone architecture, Arequipa is a charming colonial city surrounded by dramatic landscapes, including the nearby Colca Canyon.

Nazca Lines

The Nazca Lines are mysterious geoglyphs etched into the desert floor, depicting various animals and geometric shapes. They are best viewed from the air on a scenic flight.

Sacred Valley of the Incas

This fertile valley is home to numerous Inca ruins, traditional markets, and picturesque villages. It's an excellent base for exploring the surrounding Andean landscapes.

The Amazon Rainforest

Peru offers access to a significant portion of the Amazon Rainforest, with lodges and tours available for immersive wildlife encounters and rainforest exploration.

Huacachina Oasis

This picturesque desert oasis is surrounded by towering sand dunes, making it a popular spot for dune buggying and sandboarding adventures.

Paracas National Reserve

This coastal reserve is known for its stunning beaches, dramatic cliffs, and diverse birdlife, including the Humboldt penguin colony on the Ballestas Islands..

Huaraz and Cordillera Blanca

A paradise for trekkers and climbers, Huaraz is the gateway to the Cordillera Blanca, a spectacular range of snow-capped peaks, glaciers, and pristine lakes.
